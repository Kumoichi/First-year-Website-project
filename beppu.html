Beppu-Onsen

Umi-jigoku

Umi-Jigoku is one of the hot springs that was created along with the eruption of Tsurumidake at the beginning of the ninth year of the Jogan Era, about 1200 years ago. The name "Umi-Jigoku" comes from the fact that the surface of the water looks like cobalt blue like the sea. The temperature is about 98 degrees Celsius and the depth to the veins of the spring is said to be over 200 meters. The spring is acidic, and the reason why the surface of the water looks blue is because of the high concentration of iron sulfate in the hot spring water. Usually, onsen is for people to get in an relax, but for this one it is too hot for people to touch. So this onsen is just for people to watch. This is popular because the color is cool and something unique. And a lot of people take pictures here.

Chinoike-Jigoku

Blood Pond Hell is the oldest natural hell in Japan,and can be described in one word: "red hot mud pond. Red hot mud containing iron oxide, magnesium oxide, etc.,which are naturally produced by chemical reactions under high temperature and high pressure underground, erupts from the strata and accumulates,turning the whole pond red. This is the most iconic beppu-jigoku-onsen. Since Jigoku means hell, this red onsen making it looks like hell. This is also to hot for people to touch. It is 78 degree Celcius. Since it is red and something unique people would like to watch that. However it might be difficult to take a picture here, because there is usually smoke and you can't really take clear picture.
